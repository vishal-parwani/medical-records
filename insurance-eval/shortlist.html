The call

Cost is no barrier · least friction wins. The ranking numbers below were built on value (fewer riders = cheaper) — useful history, but not your deciding factor any more. With money off the table and every rider available on every plan, the call is: buy HDFC Ergo Optima Secure as a single ₹1–2cr base, fully loaded (Unlimited Restore + Optima Wellbeing OPD/dental + PED-reduction) — near-maximal coverage and the market's least claim-time friction (best settlement, best value-payout, fewest complaints), which is the friction that actually hurts when you're in hospital. Choose Tata Medicare Premier instead if you'd rather have the shortest waiting clock applied to every one of your conditions with nothing bolted on (24-mo PED, all in-built) — the lowest waiting-period friction. Niva ReAssure 2.0 is the only one that can remove the PED wait entirely, but it has the busiest claims desk — so it trades waiting-friction for claim-desk-friction. My lead pick for you: HDFC.

How to structure the ₹1 crore

With premium no barrier and least friction the goal, this is now clear-cut.

Recommended · least friction

One single ₹1 crore base go ₹2cr if offered

  • One policy, one claim desk, one renewal — nothing to coordinate when someone's in hospital.
  • No deductible mechanics — a super top-up only pays after its deductible is exhausted, which invites disputes about whether/when that happened.
  • Bonus, restoration and OPD apply to the whole ₹1cr, not fragmented across two layers.
  • Since cost is off the table, size up to ₹2cr / infinite SI for headroom against ~11.5% medical inflation.
Only if cost mattered

₹50L base + ₹50L super top-up

  • Saves ~30–40% of premium — its only advantage, now irrelevant to you.
  • Adds friction: two policies, a deductible layer, split benefits. The opposite of your goal.
  • Skip it.
Cost is no barrier · least friction is the goal. That reframes the whole shortlist: the value case that lifted Tata & ManipalCigna (fewer riders = cheaper) no longer decides it. What decides it now is the friction table above — buy every rider you want on any plan, and choose on claims-desk reliability + waiting-period exposure + how much is in-built. See "The call" below.

Before you sign

  1. Pull live quotes with riders included for Tata, ManipalCigna (+Prime Plus) and HDFC (+Optima Wellbeing +PED rider) at ₹1cr, Chennai PIN, exact DOBs — so you compare fully-loaded totals, not base premiums. My rider figures are modelled; the quotes settle it.
  2. Declare every condition in writing for all four members — non-disclosure is the top reason claims are voided, and full disclosure starts the 60-month moratorium clock.
  3. Confirm on the CIS: no proportionate deduction on associated charges, suite not re-capped, and the exact OPD/dental & PED-rider costs (the numbers that decide this).
  4. Verify Apollo / Kauvery / MIOT / SIMS are in-network on each insurer's own locator, with Cashless Everywhere live.
  5. Decide the OPD limit you actually want — a bigger OPD rider raises HDFC/ICICI/Niva further and widens Tata/ManipalCigna's value lead.
  6. Use the 30-day free-look to read the wording end-to-end; return for a full refund if anything contradicts the sales promise.
